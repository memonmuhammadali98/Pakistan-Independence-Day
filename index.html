Exploring Pakistan

Pakistan is a nation endowed with a rich history, vibrant culture, and remarkable natural beauty. From the towering mountain ranges in the north to the expansive deserts of the south, it is a land characterized by diverse landscapes and enduring traditions. The people of Pakistan are renowned for their warmth, resilience, and strong cultural values. Guided by a vision of unity and progress, Pakistan continues to advance while proudly preserving its heritage and national identity.

Pakistan's Historical Journey

1857

First War of Independence

The First War of Independence began, igniting the spark for freedom from British rule.

1906

Formation of Muslim League

The All India Muslim League was founded to represent Muslim interests in British India.

1940

Lahore Resolution

The Pakistan Resolution was passed in Lahore, demanding a separate homeland for Muslims.

1947

Independence of Pakistan

Pakistan gained independence on August 14 under the leadership of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah.

1956

First Constitution

Pakistan became the Islamic Republic by adopting its first constitution on March 23.

1965

Second War with India

Pakistan and India fought a war over Kashmir, ending with the Tashkent Agreement.

1971

Fall of Dhaka

The separation of East Pakistan led to the creation of Bangladesh after civil war.

1973

New Constitution

A new constitution was passed, establishing a parliamentary system in Pakistan.

National Heroes of Pakistan

Quaid-e-Azam

Founder of Pakistan

Leader of the Pakistan Movement and the first Governor-General of Pakistan.

Allama Iqbal

Poet of the East

Visionary who dreamed of Pakistan and inspired the youth with his poetry.

Fatima Jinnah

Mother of the Nation

Quaid-e-Azam's sister and a strong advocate for women's rights in Pakistan.

Liaquat Ali Khan

First Prime Minister

He was the first Prime Minister of Pakistan and played a major role in early governance.

Sir Syed Ahmed Khan

Muslim Reformer & Educator

Founder of Aligarh Movement, he laid the foundation for modern Muslim education in the subcontinent.

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan

Father of Nuclear Program

Key figure behind Pakistan's nuclear tests in 1998, ensuring national defense.
